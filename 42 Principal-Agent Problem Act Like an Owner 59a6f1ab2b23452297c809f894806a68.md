# **委托-代理问题：像所有者一样思考和行动**

## 委托人与代理人的激励往往并不一致

**Nivi：** 我们来谈谈委托-代理问题（principal-agent problem）。

**Naval：** 委托-代理问题是微观经济学中一个极其重要的概念，它几乎无处不在。

The principal-agent problem is a very important concept in microeconomics. It comes up everywhere.

委托人（principal）是所有者，代理人（agent）是雇员。代理人代表委托人行事，但代理人有自己的利益动机，而这些动机并不总是与委托人的利益一致。

The principal is the owner; the agent is the employee. The agent acts on behalf of the principal. But agents have their own incentives, which don't always align with the principal's incentives.

## 代理人的激励机制与委托人截然不同

一个典型的例子是房产中介。他们应该帮你以最高价格卖出房子。但他们赚取的是基于成交价的佣金。为了最后几个百分点的溢价，耗费大量时间和精力去磨价，对他们来说并不划算。他们宁愿尽快成交，尽快转向下一个客户。他们真正的动机不是帮你卖出最高价，而是尽快促成交易。

A classic example is a real estate agent. They're supposed to get you the best price for your house. But they make a commission based on the sale price. Squeezing out the last few percentage points of a higher price isn't worth their time and effort. They'd rather close the deal quickly and move on to the next client. Their real incentive isn't to get you the best price; it's to close deals fast.

同样，一个理财顾问管理着你的钱，但他们的动机可能是频繁操作以赚取手续费，即便这对你并无益处。

Likewise, a financial advisor managing your money may be incentivized to churn the portfolio and collect fees, even if that's not in your interest.

## 代理人在该冒险时过于保守，在不该冒险时又过于激进

由于代理人不需要承担决策的全部后果，他们要么冒险过度，要么规避过度。

Because agents don't bear the full consequences of their decisions, they can end up taking too much risk or too little.

如果我用你的钱投资，赚钱了我拿奖金，亏钱了顶多丢工作——那我可能会承担过高的风险。我享有上行收益，你承担下行损失。

If I'm investing your money and I get a bonus when I make money but only lose my job if I lose a lot, I might take excessive risks. I capture the upside; you bear the downside.

反过来，如果我作为一名管理者，冒险成功不会带来晋升，冒险失败却可能丢饭碗，那我就会过度保守。下行风险由我承担，上行收益却被你拿走。

Or if I'm a manager who won't get promoted for a risk that pays off but will get fired for one that fails, I'll be overly conservative. I bear the downside; you miss the upside.

这正与纳西姆·塔勒布（Nassim Taleb）所强调的"切肤之痛"（skin in the game）密切相关——代理人所承担的风险，与他们用自己的钱、拿自己的命运下注时的风险截然不同。

This is closely related to Nassim Taleb's concept of "skin in the game." Agents take different risks than they would if it were their own money and their own reputation on the line.

## 激励机制决定一切

查理·芒格（Charlie Munger）说过：**"告诉我激励机制是什么，我就能告诉你结果是什么。"** 激励机制是解释人类行为的最强大的力量。

Charlie Munger says, "Show me the incentive, and I'll show you the outcome." Incentives are the most powerful force in explaining human behavior.

如果你想解决委托-代理问题，就必须从根本上修正激励机制。

If you want to fix the principal-agent problem, you have to fix the incentives.

## 如果你表现得像个所有者，你终将成为所有者

作为委托人，你需要给代理人以股权，让他们与你共享经营成果，从而实现利益的对齐。你希望他们拥有一部分结果，而不只是执行指令。

As a principal, you want to give agents equity in the business so their incentives align with yours. You want them to own a piece of the outcome, not just execute instructions.

如果你是一名代理人，你能做的最重要的事情就是**像委托人一样思考和行动**。像所有者一样思考，而不是像租客一样思考。所有者关注长期价值，关注工作质量本身而非表面，承担结果的责任而非仅仅关注努力的过程。

If you are an agent, the most important thing you can do is to think and act like a principal. Think like an owner, not like a renter. Owners care about the long term. Owners care about the quality of the work itself, not just its appearance. Owners take responsibility for outcomes, not just effort.

这也是你成为委托人的必经之路。**如果你思考和行动都像一个所有者，假以时日，你必将成为一个所有者。**

This is also how you train yourself to become a principal over time. If you think and act like an owner, it's only a matter of time until you become one.
